TradingView vs Koyfin: Which Fits Your Mining Workflow?

The honest answer for 2026: most active resource investors use both. Route discovery through TradingView when relative strength, seasonality, and volatility regimes matter—common in gold and silver equities. Route validation through Koyfin when you must compare balance sheets, capex cycles, and leverage across peers before adding risk.

If you must pick one, match the tool to your failure mode: if you chronically buy broken charts, fix the chart process first (TradingView). If you chronically buy cheap stocks that stay cheap because of debt, fix the fundamentals process first (Koyfin). How to Screen for Mining Stocks: Filters That Actually Matter

Use these building blocks inside whichever mining stock screener you choose—they translate across TradingView, Koyfin, and broker scanners:

  • Sector and industry: Start with materials, metals & mining, gold, silver, copper, or diversified miners—then narrow to producers vs explorers based on risk tolerance.
  • Valuation: Screen EV/EBITDA, price to book, and EV/resource or price to NAV where data exists; discard outliers until you verify one-off items.
  • Balance sheet: Debt to equity, interest coverage, and liquidity ratios—mining downturns punish leverage.
  • Quality of operations: Where available, filter on cash costs, production growth, and reserve replacement—not just headline EPS.
  • Liquidity and float: Minimum average dollar volume so you can exit; critical for juniors.
  • Jurisdiction: Use country or exchange filters (TSX, ASX, LSE, BVL) to align with your political risk budget.

After the mechanical screen, read the last two earnings calls—or at least the transcript summary on Seeking Alpha—before you size up. For execution and locates on thin names, route through Interactive Brokers once your conviction is real.

TradingView vs Koyfin: which is better for mining investors?

TradingView wins technical screening and global charting; Koyfin wins multi-year financial history and clean comps. Use TradingView for timing and Koyfin for conviction—see our dedicated section above.

Can I screen for mining stocks for free?
