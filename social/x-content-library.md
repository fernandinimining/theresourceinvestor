### Thread 1 — Copper is the New Oil

**Hook:** Electrification isn’t a tech story—it’s a **copper-tonnes** story. If you’re betting on grids, EVs, and data centers, you’re implicitly betting on who gets paid to move rock.

1. Power density rises with renewables + storage buildouts. More copper per MW than legacy thermal—**demand isn’t linear with GDP**.

2. Mine supply is lumpy: permits, grades, strikes, weather. **Deficits show up in price** when inventories can’t buffer shocks.

3. Equity upside isn’t “copper up = miners up.” It’s **cost position + jurisdiction + balance sheet**—otherwise you’re renting beta.

4. Watch treatment/refining charges and scrap—**they telegraph** whether the physical market is tight or just financialized.

5. Majors vs mid-tiers: majors give durability; mid-tiers give torque. Your portfolio should know which job each name is doing.

6. The “new oil” framing is about **scarcity of timely supply**, not hype. The dataset to watch is **project pipelines**, not day-trader sentiment.

### Thread 2 — Gold at $3,200

**Hook:** Round-number headlines grab attention; **margin structures** decide returns. At $3,200 gold, the winners aren’t automatic—they’re the lowest-cost, cleanest balance sheets.

1. Start with **AISC bands**—who actually converts ounces to cash after sustaining capex?

2. Compare **EV/oz reserves**—high spot gold can hide mediocre assets if costs creep.

3. Hedging matters: a “cheap” miner can be **an option with a ceiling** if forwards are heavy.

4. Jurisdiction risk doesn’t disappear when price rises—**royalties and policy** can eat the upside.

5. Majors vs juniors: juniors bring discovery torque; majors bring **duration and dividends**—mix intentionally.

6. Sentiment peaks invite dilution and M&A. Watch issuance—**share count is the silent thief** of per-share value.

6. Combine fund data with **your** risk limits—copying without sizing discipline is a portfolio bug, not a feature.

### Thread 6 — Mining Dividend Aristocrats

**Hook:** “Dividend” in mining isn’t like consumer staples—**it’s a cyclical promise**. The best payers pair yield with balance-sheet flexibility, not just a high headline rate.

6. Screen for names that maintained or grew distributions **after** prior downturns—that’s the aristocrat smell test in resources.
