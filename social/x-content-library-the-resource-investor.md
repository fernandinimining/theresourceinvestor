### Thread 1 — “Why Copper is the New Oil”

**1/7** Copper isn’t oil—but the *constraints* rhyme: long lead times, concentrated supply, and demand tied to macro shifts. Here’s a data-first look at why copper dominates the energy transition. 🧵 Not financial advice. #Copper #Mining  

**2/7** EVs use far more copper per vehicle than ICE cars; charging + grid reinforcement add incremental demand. Industry studies cluster around high multipliers—exact numbers vary by model and region.  

**3/7** Mine supply isn’t a dial: grades have declined for decades, and new projects face permitting, water, and capex inflation. “Nameplate” capacity rarely equals smooth output.  

**4/7** Scrap responds to price—secondary supply can ease short squeezes. Structural balance still depends on how fast demand shifts vs how fast new mines arrive.  

**5/7** Geography matters: Chile & Peru matter for global balances; any disruption moves the forward curve faster than headlines suggest.  

**6/7** Mine-by-mine work beats ticker chasing: recovery, power, by-products (moly, silver), and sustaining capex decide who keeps margin when costs rise.  

**7/7** Full article—evidence, charts, and a clear framework: https://globalminingintel.com (add your article path). Follow for more copper research. #Copper

### Thread 2 — “Gold at $3,200”

**1/8** Gold near record highs invites two mistakes: pure fear narratives and pure “money printing” memes. Here’s a cleaner framework—central banks, real yields, and flows. Not financial advice. #Gold  

**2/8** Official-sector buying has been a major story: World Gold Council annual data shows strong net purchases in recent years—check the latest annual/quarterly tables for exact tonnes.  

**3/8** Real yields matter: when inflation-adjusted bond returns compress, gold’s opportunity cost falls *ceteris paribus*—a core valuation channel, not a day-trading rule.  

**4/8** Jewelry + bar/coin demand interact with price—high prices can depress some physical channels while investment demand rotates.  

**5/8** Bull case (conceptual): sustained CB diversification + macro uncertainty + strong investment flows. Bear case: higher real yields, risk-on rotation, profit-taking.  

**6/8** Miners ≠ gold: operating leverage cuts both ways—AISC, hedging, and jurisdiction can swamp spot moves.  

**7/8** Use scenarios, not prophecies: model gold at a band, stress FX and costs, then ask which miners survive the downside.  

**8/8** Read the full valuation + scenario note: https://globalminingintel.com (article path). #Gold #Mining

### Thread 3 — “Lithium Crash Recovery”

**1/7** Post-boom lithium is a cost-curve fight, not a meme. Which miners survive depends on ore type, chemistry, energy, and balance sheets. 🧵 Not financial advice. #Lithium  

**2/7** Spodumene vs brine vs clay: each has different capex, opex, and technical risk—compare *all-in* economics, not headline grades.  

**3/7** Offtake and pricing matter: contract structures can lag spot, smoothing revenue—or hiding pain.  

**4/7** Balance sheet liquidity wins in troughs: refinancings dilute; hedging can help—or hurt.  

**5/7** Integration downstream (hydroxide/carbonate) can add margin—or complexity. Execution risk is real.  

**6/7** Recovery isn’t “back to 2022”—it’s a slower demand slope with supply rationalization. Map survivors by cost quartile.

### Thread 4 — “Hedge Funds Accumulating Miners”

**1/8** 13-F filings don’t tell you *why* funds buy miners—but they show positioning size and style shifts. Here’s how to read them without fooling yourself. 🧵 Not financial advice.  

**2/7** 13-F is quarterly, stale, and aggregated—you see stocks, not options, not non-U.S. wrappers perfectly. Use it as a screen, not gospel.  

**3/7** Look for *changes* quarter-over-quarter: new positions vs adds vs exits—magnitude matters more than celebrity names.  

**4/7** Macro funds may trade miners as beta on copper/gold; specialists may drill into NAV. Different holding periods.  

**5/7** Pair filings with miner fundamentals: rising ownership + weak balance sheet can still end badly if metal rolls over.  

**6/7** Institutional thesis often centers on FCF inflection, reserve quality, and jurisdictional risk reduction—not “story.”  

**7/7** Watch red flags: repeated misses, capex overruns, and guidance cuts—filings won’t save bad operations.  

**8/8** Full article on institutional patterns + checklist: https://globalminingintel.com (article path). #Mining #Commodities

### Thread 5 — “Silver’s Solar Boom”

**1/7** Silver is split: PV and electronics on one side, monetary/investment on the other. The solar channel is real—but it’s not the whole book. 🧵 Not financial advice. #Silver  

**2/7** Solar loadings per watt have improved over time—technology changes silver intensity *per GW*; track both deployment and thrifting.  

**3/7** Industrial demand can rise with installations; recycling and inventory buffers also influence the market.  

**4/7** Silver often trades with gold on macro days and copper on growth days—expect correlation regime shifts.  

**5/7** Primary silver mines are rare; much supply is by-product—cost curves are intertwined with lead/zinc/copper systems.  

**6/7** Stock pickers: separate “silver price beta” from “actual silver revenue share”—they’re not the same.  

**7/7** Deep dive on solar + silver balance: https://globalminingintel.com (article path). #Silver #Mining

### Thread 6 — “Mining Dividend Aristocrats”

**1/8** “High yield” in mining can mean a healthy payout—or a warning sign. Here’s how to compare dividends without fooling yourself. 🧵 Not financial advice. #Mining  

**2/7** Start with FCF, not GAAP net income: sustaining capex and working capital swings distort earnings.  

**3/7** Payout ratio vs cycle: paying out most of FCF in peak years sets up cuts when metal falls—check history.  

**4/7** Gold vs copper producers: different margin structures; by-product credits can flatter copper cash costs.  

**5/7** Hedging: locked-in prices stabilize dividends—or cap upside. Read the notes.  

**6/7** Total return beats yield alone: dividend + (or minus) price change + dilution from equity raises.  

**7/7** Jurisdiction and political risk can erase dividends overnight—boards cut fast when leverage rises.
